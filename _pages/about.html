Hi, I'm Lewis. I am a Computational Scientist in the Division For Climate Modeling and Air Pollution at the Norwegian Meterological Institute in Oslo. My work is primarily in climate and air pollution model evaluation. I contribute to the Python package pyaerocom and the European CAMS project.

I completed my PhD in Statistics from the Department of Applied Mathematics and Statistics at Colorado School of Mines, USA in December 2021. My thesis foucsed on developing spatial statistics tools for analyzing massive spatial data sets to study the Earth system. A primary component of my PhD was as a Research Assistant at the National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR) in Boulder, Colorado, USA. There I developed and released scalable machine learning models which allow users to compute with hundreds-of-millions of observations and reduced computation times by 75%.
